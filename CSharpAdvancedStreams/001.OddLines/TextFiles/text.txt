Microsoft Developer Network, widely referred to by its abbreviation,
MSDN, is the portion of Microsoft responsible for managing the firm's
relationship with developers and testers, such as hardware developers 
interested in the operating system (OS), and software developers 
developing on the various OS platforms or using the API and/or scripting 
languages of Microsoft's applications. The relationship management is situated 
in assorted media: web sites, newsletters, developer conferences, trade media, 
blogs and DVD distribution. The life cycle of the relationships ranges from legacy 
support through evangelizing potential offerings.
